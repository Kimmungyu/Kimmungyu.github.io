Summary

EMBOLD For Architects

Bad Software architecture leads to issues. This is something every Architect knows. But nevertheless, a lot of companies are struggling with designing and maintaining great software architectures. This is why we put Software Design first when creating Embold, to provide Architects with all the tools required to design and deliver high quality software.

Customer Stories

Identify and solve Design Problems

You know your software architecture is not optimal and foresee that this will cause challenges in the future? You fear that quality KPIs such as maintainability, robustness or extensibility will be affected by the current architecture? With Embold we are providing you with a toolset that will make your life as an architect much more enjoyable, by helping you to identify and solve Design Problems easily.

With Embold, you can analyse your code’s structural design against our unique set of anti-patterns on class, function and method levels. These anti-patterns have been created both from books and research, and from our own experience in writing and analyzing business critical software. Especially in combination with our unique rating system this can help you to identify and solve design problems in software projects with much less time required compared to other tools or reading the source code.

Maintain a high architecture standard

You are in charge of the Software Design, but don’t have the time or resources to ensure architecture standards are followed consequently? You have designed the architecture a long time back and are unsure if it is still followed through? We have developed Embold to help you overcoming architecture challenges and maintaining a high architecture standard.

With our design anti-patterns and rating system, you as an architect can see immediately when a certain software change introduces a structure design issue and react upon it before the architecture problems cumulate. Very often it is difficult to keep track on the overall software architecture during the development, especially in agile environments. This way larger architectural issues are being introduced and identified too late, leading to costly and time-consuming analysis and re-factoring.

Derive Refactoring Recommendations

You know a certain class or module is not architected optimally, leading to high maintenance and bug proneness? You fear the effort and risk with analyzing the component, proposing and implementing a refactoring? Proposing a refactoring is typically a challenging task. The current state of the module or component that is to be refactored must be assessed, which includes analyzing the structure of those together with internal and external dependencies.

With the Embold dependency plot this becomes a much easier task, as ingoing and outgoing dependencies of components can be visualized up to as many levels as required. With the partitioning algorithm we even go one step further and propose possible refactorings of components using graph and NLP analysis.

Refactoring

Embold finds anti patterns in code and visualizes them in its recommendation engine. It helps to understand the structur of unknown code through NLP analysis, to get a better understanding of the code.

The connections between methods and functions are shown, making it easy to find a functional unit in a class and extract it. This saves a big amount of the analysis time and makes refactoring faster and less exhausting for developers and architects.

Project Quality Awareness

Embold, helps you to understand the code and architectural quality in your code landscape and projects. It shows at a glance through the Gamma Score, which projects are suffering from high technical debt on code level.

By this you can embolden your developers with better resource allocation and create a positive eustress instead of vicious cycles that burn developers and project milestones. This favors a higher satisfaction of customers and developers.

Code Review

Embold provides the ability to do side by side reviews between code changes. Here architects can explain Anti-Patterns to developers and keep the design clean.

Creating a better awareness and mutual understanding, between developers, architects and technical project managers directly at the code level. Saving you time while reviewing, that you can spent more time with creating.

Summary

EMBOLD For Financial Industry

The digital transformation of the financial industry and its business models is at its peak. There is a high pressure to change due to potential competition from technology companies, regulation, low interest rates and cost pressure. Speed is becoming increasingly important in order to react to new competition or new requirements. This can quickly lead to costly errors and limit expandability.

At the same time, many software systems have been in use for decades and are no longer sufficient for the requirements of digitization. Grown legacy systems and company takeovers have led to a very complex system landscape and it is very time-consuming to create transparency across all systems used. Quality assurance often falls by the wayside. Embold has helped all our customers in the financial industry with those challenges.

Customer Stories

Core Financial and Insurance Systems

Embold has been used to analyze core financial systems such as core systems for banking, investments funds, VCs, insurance, leasing systems and more. It creates transparency, accelerates software development and ensures high software quality standards, also with suppliers.

Legacy Systems

Embold can analyze even large legacy systems and provide quality recommendations quickly. This helps to understand old systems efficiently and can support in transformation or replacement projects

Shadow IT

Embold can quickly analyze shadow software in specialist departments. This brings immediate transparency over all software assets and helps to assess their inherent risks. This helped our customer to address highest risk systems first.

Outsourced software development

Embold helps our customers to ensure the software quality of their service providers stays high on multiple key performance indicators such as maintainability, robustness and extensibility. For many customers, Embold is a part of the Supplier Quality Assurance process.

Supplier Management

Embold enables you to give a quality score and trend to vendors and their teams. By adding a price tag, you can see where you can get the best quality value.

This data is combined by our BI reporting, if available, enabling you to have a strong portfolio view about vendors working for you, to always choose the best value for you.

Quality improvement

As a first to improve the quality of your project the hotspots are identified. Embold takes the developer activities as well as the tickets that effected certain components into account to create a risk metric. The Risk metric combined with a high number of code and design issues and bad metrics marks a certain component as hotspots in Embold.

Together with the development teams, those hotspots can be addressed and a refactoring ticket for the next Sprint can be created. The teams normally prioritize those areas that are or will be under active development in the next sprints. By this your repositories and projects will always have a positive trend and your releases rollout like expected.

Communication - Support through visualisation and fair KPIs

When it comes to topics like technical debt and its effects, often non-technical persons have a hard time to understand, even if they are responsible for the resources tackling the task in the engine room directly or indirectly. Embold Visuals like our recommendation engine, heat map, trend reports and partitioning plot, let everybody in the meeting fast grasp the concept of the problem at hand. This helps highly to enable the smart thinking people in your company that are not technical to contribute to a solution or at least to understand the issue.

Plus it helps to weld your team together with its managers and bridges the frustrating gap in day to day problem communication. Technical colleagues feel better understood and a more efficient communication is possible.

Since Embold uses KPIs that agregate issues that the developers can link back to a line in the code base, Embold KPIs are actionable for managers as well as developers. When you as a manager say that you need to get the KPI “Accuracy” better throughout your portfolio, because customers complaint about problems with “Accurancy”, then developers know exactly what they must do. Line by line. No misunderstanding, just brilliant execution at a fraction of the time.

This is transparent and fair to everybody who is willing to commit to the process, which builds a team trust as well as goal alignment penetrating different levels of an organisation unseen for most people in their complete work life.
